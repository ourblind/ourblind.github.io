Community Guidelines

Following are the common Community Guidelines for OurBlind on all its platforms. Abiding by the platforms’ Terms & Conditions is implied and required. These guidelines do not intend to cover every case, but to serve as a common framework. Each platform may have additional items due to its specific nature.

Rule 1: Accessibility Policy

This community must remain accessible for members with a variety of disabilities. Please do your best to do the following:

  • Writing image descriptions is required for all images, GIFs, and custom emoji you post in this community. Using the alt text feature built into the platform is preferred, but you may also include it in the body of the post as an alternative if needed.

  • Standard emoji are screen reader accessible and thus are allowed without descriptions, but please be mindful to avoid emoji spam.

  • Audio content must also be transcribed if it does not already have a transcript or accessible subtitles, and you must describe both the visuals and audio for videos you post.

  • No special characters or special fonts are allowed in your messages or in your username, as they are not screen reader accessible.

  • No Unicode Braille, sim Braille, or ASCII Braille are allowed in your messages or your username, as they are also not fully screen reader accessible.

If you have questions about how to do any of these things, please feel free to ask for help. Do feel free to search the web about how to use the platform’s alt text feature.

If you want to get feedback about your project from blind people, post a survey you are conducting about blind people, promote a blog or social media page you have made, advertize a product you are selling, advertize an event or another community, or similar activities you may not post this in this community without mod approval. If you violate this rule and post your survey or promotion in any other channel without mod approval, or DM our members/solicit DMs from our members about your project without mod approval, your post will be removed and other potential moderator actions may be taken.

Rule 3: Adult Content

This community is welcoming to people of all ages, so must respect the needs and interests of its diverse members.

Rule 3.1: OurBlind Discord server

Some channels are age restricted and designated for discussion of adult topics. Adult topics are not allowed in any of the non-18+ channels, and minors may not enter the 18+ channels.

Directly posting or linking to pornography or promoting illegal activity is not allowed on this server, even in the 18+ channels.

Rule 3.2: RBlind.com Lemmy instance

Sensitive mature content should be marked NSFW. Off Topic NSFW content will not be tolerated in communities hosted on this instance. Members may subscribe to and post in NSFW communities hosted by other instances if they wish, so long as they are in compliance with the rules of those communities.

Rule 3.3 - r/Blind Reddit community

Sensitive mature content should be marked NSFW. Off Topic NSFW content will not be tolerated in this community.

Rule 4: Medical and Legal Advice

Do not give medical or legal advice such as medication or supplements to take or medical treatments to undergo. Non-medical recommendations such as types of sunglasses or assistive tools are OK, and sharing your own experiences with treatments and surgeries is also acceptable, but we are not doctors and should not make medical recommendations

Above all else, this is the most important rule. It‚Äôs okay to disagree with someone and even have arguments, however, every interaction you have here must be carried out with respect for your fellow members.

  • Make space for others to participate in conversation bu using different channels, threads, posts or DMs.

Rule 6: Moderation

Most rule violations will result in a notice, then potentially a time-out given to the offending user. Subsequent misbehavior or particularly egregious violations of community rules may result in other punitive actions against offending users up to and including the issuance of a ban.

Moderators ultimately retain the right to determine what constitutes a rule violation such as misconduct, disrespectful behavior, Etc.
